"What’s it all about?" was recorded in Leicester at the time of the invasion of Iraq, April 2003 and has two jobs:

1. To give you four good songs to sing to other people

  • "Tomorrow" was written by Peggy Seeger and is sung here by Sheila Mosley and Pete Burnham.
  • "Battle in Britain" was penned during Margaret Thatcher’s time but is still relevant. It was written by Steve Parker and this recording is taken from his album "All those Moons".
  • "I asked" royalties to CND, written by Chris Gimlett, sung by Sheila Mosley
  • "Harvest of Men" was written and performed by Steve Thomason.
2. To raise funds for three groups (these are Sheila’s choices):
  • Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament
  • Worker-communist Party of Iraq
  • Stop the War Coalition
Tracks 1,3,4 were recorded by Neil Segrott - www.neilsegrott.com
Track 2 was recorded by Kev Reverb, Memphis Studio

Many thanks to everyone, including the CND banner-makers, Pete Burnham for the photos and Jessie for help with the insert.
